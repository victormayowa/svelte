Protocol Development
A protocol is the plan or set of steps to be followed in a study.  A protocol for a systematic review should describe the rationale for the review, the objectives, and the methods that will be used to locate, select, and critically appraise studies, and to collect and analyze data from the included studies.

Advantages to producing a protocol
Helps keep you on track 
Helps you describe and explain your methods 
Provide constructive criticism 
Helps your reader understand what you have done


What to Include in a Protocol?
Conceptual discussion of the problem
Review questions and objectives
Search strategy
Study selection criteria
Study quality assessments
Data extraction procedure
Data synthesis procedure
Project timeline
